Monolith vs Microservices

Monolithic Architecture

A monolithic application is built and deployed as a single unit. All business logic, APIs, and data access layers are contained in one application.

Advantages:

* Simple development and deployment
* Easier debugging
* Lower operational complexity
* Suitable for startups and small teams

Disadvantages:

* Difficult to scale individual components
* Large codebase becomes harder to maintain
* Slower deployment cycles

Best Use Cases:

* MVPs
* Small teams
* Products with limited complexity

Microservices Architecture

Microservices split an application into multiple independently deployable services. Each service owns a specific business capability.

Advantages:

* Independent deployment
* Better scalability
* Team autonomy
* Technology flexibility

Disadvantages:

* Higher operational complexity
* Distributed system challenges
* Increased infrastructure costs

Best Use Cases:

* Large-scale applications
* Multiple engineering teams
* High scalability requirements

Decision Guidelines

Choose Monolith when:

* Team size is small
* Product is in early stages
* Development speed is the priority

Choose Microservices when:

* Independent scaling is required
* Multiple teams work simultaneously
* System complexity is high
